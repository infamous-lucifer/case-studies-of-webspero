The client

Prosperity Financial Group: strategic wealth management for individuals and businesses

A US-based financial services firm providing comprehensive financial planning, investment advisory, and wealth management. Serving both individual investors and businesses, Prosperity focuses on strategic portfolio growth and long-term financial guidance in a highly scrutinised, trust-driven market where establishing authority is critical to acquisition.

The challenge

Competing in financial SEO: high scrutiny, high intent, and deep technical demands

Missing high-intent conversions
The existing keyword portfolio lacked coverage for conversion-driving queries. The site was missing the exact moments when qualified buyers were actively searching for financial advisory.
The Page 2 bottleneck
Strong informational pages were stranded in positions 11–30. Without specific on-page intent alignment, these "striking distance" pages could not break through to Page 1 visibility.
Technical crawl inefficiencies
Underlying technical debt and crawl limitations were preventing Google from fully discovering and indexing the site's most valuable content, silently suppressing domain performance.
Scattered link equity
A fragmented internal linking architecture meant that existing domain authority was not flowing efficiently to the primary conversion targets and core service pages.
Our approach

Intent-aligned optimisation + technical crawl architecture

01
Striking distance intent optimisation
Title tags, meta descriptions, and on-page headings for pages ranking in positions 11–30 were rewritten to precisely match search intent. This transitioned existing content onto Page 1 for rapid traffic acquisition without waiting on net-new content to mature.
02
Technical indexing & schema deployment
Crawl bottlenecks were resolved and financial service structured data (schema markup) was implemented. This ensured complete indexing of core pages, improved Core Web Vitals, and qualified the site for SERP rich results to boost click-through rates.
03
Topical authority & equity distribution
In-depth content hubs were developed to establish definitive category authority. Simultaneously, internal linking paths were restructured to funnel domain authority directly from high-traffic pages to high-priority wealth management conversion targets.

Actionable insights

What financial service firms can learn from this campaign

Striking distance is the fastest ROI
Keywords sitting in positions 11–30 are your lowest-hanging fruit. Slight on-page intent adjustments can push these onto Page 1 much faster than creating and ranking net-new content.
Technical debt suppresses authority
In YMYL (Your Money or Your Life) sectors, Google demands technical perfection. Unresolved crawl inefficiencies will cap your rankings regardless of how authoritative your content is.
Link equity must be directed
Domain authority is useless if it doesn't reach your money pages. A deliberate internal linking structure acts as a plumbing system, pushing equity exactly where it is needed to drive conversions.
Organic compounds, paid expires
Paid search in finance is notoriously expensive. Building topical hubs and capturing high-intent terms organically creates a permanent acquisition asset that outlasts any ad budget.
